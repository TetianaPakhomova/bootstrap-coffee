The Best Coffee Regions

Brazil

Arabica dominates both Brazil and the world, making up about 85% of production, while Robusta accounts for the remaining 15%. Arabica is mainly produced in Minas Gerais and São Paulo, while Robusta is primarily grown in Espírito Santo.

about Brazil

India

Indian coffee is mostly grown in southern India under monsoon rainfall conditions and is known as "Indian Monsooned Coffee". The main species are Arabica and Robusta. The most common Arabica variety is S.795.

Learn more about India

Costa Rica

Costa Rican coffee beans are considered among the best in the world. Tarrazú produces the most desirable beans. The finest coffee is typically grown at altitudes of 1200–1700 meters.

Learn more about Costa Rica

Colombia

Colombia produces mild, well-balanced Arabica coffee. Average annual production is about 11.5 million bags. Coffee is mainly exported to the USA, Germany, France, Japan, and Italy.

Learn more about Colombia

Ethiopia

Ethiopian beans are categorized into Longberry, Shortberry, and Mocha. Mocha Harars are prized for complex chocolate, spice, and citrus notes.

Learn more about Ethiopia

Kenya

Central Kenya’s highlands provide ideal conditions. Known for intense flavor, full body, and pleasant aroma with cocoa notes.
